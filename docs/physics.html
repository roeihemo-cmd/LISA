Model Reference · v1

LiDAR ADAS Simulator Every mathematical & physical model behind the signal chain and the autonomous decision logic.

A single end-to-end pipeline: a Gaussian laser pulse propagates through atmosphere, returns buried in noise, is recovered by a matched filter, and its estimated range feeds a physics-based braking & steering decision. Two subsystems, one flow.

A

LiDAR Signal Chain

optical physics · DSP · detection

01
Transmitted Gaussian pulse
s(t) = exp( −(t − t0)2 / 2σ2 )
A narrow laser pulse — the reference shape used both to transmit and to matched-filter.
02
Sampling & time window
fs = 3 GHz  ·  Twin = 2Rmax / c
Receiver sampled fast enough that a pulse spans several samples across the full range window.
03
Time of flight
τ = 2R / c
Round-trip delay — the quantity the whole system exists to measure.
04
Atmospheric transmission — Beer–Lambert
ηatm(R) = e−α·2R
Two-way absorption through fog/rain. α = 0 is clear air; higher α blinds the sensor.
05
Optical radar equation
Prec = Ptx · Dr2/(4R2) · ηatm · ρ
Returned power: inverse-square spreading × atmospheric loss × target reflectivity.
06
Detector gain
A = Prec · G
Converts the tiny received optical power into an electrical signal amplitude.
07
Noisy receiver — AWGN
r(t) = A·s(t − τ) + n(t),  n ∼ 𝒩(0, σ2)
The echo arrives delayed and scaled, submerged in additive white Gaussian noise.
08
Matched filter (pulse compression)
y(t) = r(t) ⋆ s(t)
Correlation with the known pulse maximizes SNR and collapses the echo into a sharp peak.
09
Robust noise floor
nf = 1.4826 · MAD(y)
Median-absolute-deviation estimate — resistant to the peak itself skewing the threshold.
10
Detection test
SNR = peak / nf  ≥  6
Target declared present only when the compressed peak clears the noise floor by 6×.
11
Range estimate
R̂ = c · τ̂ / 2
Converts the detected peak time back into a distance.
12
Range resolution
ΔR = c / (2 fs)  ≈  5 cm
The finest distance the sampling rate can distinguish.

B

Vehicle Physics & Decision Logic

braking dynamics · reaction model · autonomous decision

01
Physical braking distance
Dbraking = v2 / (2a)
Minimum distance to stop at maximum deceleration.
a = 6.5 m/s²
02
Reaction distance
Dsafe = v·tr + buffer
Ground covered during system + reaction lag, plus a fixed safety margin.
tr = 0.8 s   buffer = 5 m
03
Total required stopping distance
Dtotal = Dbraking + Dsafe
The distance actually needed to stop safely — the real threshold.
04
Comfortable engagement threshold
Dengage = v2/(2ac) + Dsafe
React early, at a gentle deceleration rather than a last-second slam.
ac = 3.5 m/s²
05
Closing rate
Δv = vego − vtarget   (threshold 15 km/h)
A slowly-approached moving car does not warrant a full emergency stop.
06
Gap kinematics
dR/dt = vtarget − vego
The gap closes or opens by the relative speed of the two vehicles.
07
Deceleration (matches the physics)
DECEL = a · 3.6 = 23.4 km/h·s−1
On-screen stopping distance equals the displayed braking distance — no contradictions.
08
Target emergency brake
atarget = 8 m/s2  after  3 s
The "brake-threat" lead vehicle slams its brakes mid-scenario.

Autonomous Decision Tree

threat when  Dsensor < Dengage
├─ Dsensor ≥ Dtotal  and  Δv > 15  → AEB (brake to lead speed)
├─ else, adjacent lane free  → LANE MERGE
└─ else (no lane)  → AEB (last resort)
   after AEB matches a moving lead  → FOLLOW (adaptive cruise)
